There is a particular fear in the first piece &mdash; that whatever follows will be measured against it, that the second piece will need to justify the existence of the first, that some imagined reader is waiting for a coherent project to begin announcing itself.

This is, of course, not how anyone reads. Real readers arrive in the middle, find one thing they like, and read it. They do not audit the trajectory. They do not weigh a piece against its predecessors. The pressure to "begin well" is almost entirely manufactured by the author, alone, in advance.

So this is a beginning that refuses to perform a beginning. A note about the practice of writing slowly &mdash; the only practice I am genuinely interested in keeping up. The site exists because writing without publishing has its own kind of cost. Things written that nobody reads tend to drift back into being unwritten.

Some part of any working practice is the agreement to be read, even just a little. That agreement is what this site is for.
